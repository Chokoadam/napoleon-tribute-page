Timeline of Napoleon Bonaparte's Life:

  • 1769 - Born in Ajaccio, Corsica, on August 15th.
  • 1779 - A future general: Enjoyed reading about history, was good at maths, however, he was also made fun of in school for his Corsican accent. He then attended military school to learn about weapons and horseback riding and eventually became a commander to command soldiers.
  • 1792 - The first coalition, which lasted from 1792-1797. With Napoleon's great strategic prowess, he led France to a victory against Britain, Russia, Prussia, Spain, Holland, and Austria.
  • 1799 - Napoleon takes power of France. With the support of his people, he took power and reinstated the government, banks, and policies of France.
  • 1800 - The Deuxième Campagne d’Italie - Austria would not accept Napoleon as ruler, so Napoleon crossed the Alps and surprised Austria and defeated them in a decisive battle.
  • 1804 - Napoleon was crowned emperor.
  • 1805 - Between 1805-1810, Napoleon won many battles and wars to extend his empire in Europe.
  • 1812 - Napoleon went to war with Russia, he got as far as Moscow as the extreme cold proved to be his biggest enemy. This resulted in Napoleon's first loss.
  • 1814 - The fall of Napoleon's empire. After the loss to the Russians, France's enemies invaded France and despite a few victories, Napoleon abandoned his position as emperor and his victors sent him into exile to an island called Elba.
  • 1815 - Napoleon escaped and returned to Paris to regain his rule over France. His enemies were not happy however and they defeated him in the battle of Waterloo and exiled him again to a more discrete island called St. Helena.
  • 1821 - After spending his last years being closely monitored by the British, and living in a dark, damp house, Napoleon finally died on May 5th, 1821.
